加缪：荒诞与反抗

A Turn in the Road · Alfred Sisley

加缪不是存在主义者，虽然所有人都这么叫他。他其实是在批判存在主义——批判那些无法忍受荒诞、非要给宇宙找一个意义的人。

他的核心论断很直接：人有一种根本的需求，要求这个世界是有意义的、可理解的。但宇宙对这个需求保持沉默。这个冲突就是”荒诞”。

大多数哲学家遇到这里会做两件事：一是自杀（字面意义），二是”哲学上的自杀”——跳跃到宗教或某种超验，用信仰填平那道沟。加缪说这两种都是逃跑。

他的答案是：不逃。承认荒诞，同时继续活着，继续反抗。

西西弗的故事——神明罚他推石头上山，石头滚下来，永远重复——加缪说，我们必须想象西西弗是快乐的。不是因为他找到了意义，而是因为他的反抗本身就是他的东西。那块石头是他的。

这和奥勒留有一种奇怪的对称：奥勒留说”控制你能控制的，接受其余”，加缪说”接受没有什么可以控制，然后依然选择”。两个人都在战场上写作，都在面对真实的混乱，都拒绝垮掉。

不同的是：奥勒留有宇宙秩序（斯多葛的 logos），加缪什么都没有，只有人自己。

后者更孤独，但也更诚实。

Camus wasn’t an existentialist, though everyone called him one. He was actually critiquing existentialism — critiquing those who couldn’t tolerate the absurd and had to find a meaning for the universe at any cost.

His core argument is direct: humans have a deep need for the world to make sense, to be comprehensible. The universe maintains a complete silence in response to that need. The collision between the need and the silence is the absurd.

Most philosophers who reach this point do one of two things: literal suicide, or what Camus calls “philosophical suicide” — leaping to religion or some transcendent framework, using faith to fill the gap. Camus says both are running away.

His answer is: don’t run. Acknowledge the absurd, and keep living anyway. Keep revolting.

Sisyphus — condemned by the gods to roll his boulder up the hill, watch it roll back, repeat forever — Camus says we must imagine Sisyphus happy. Not because he found meaning, but because the revolt itself is his. The boulder is his.

There’s a strange symmetry with Marcus Aurelius here: Aurelius says “control what you can, accept the rest.” Camus says “accept that there is nothing you can control, and choose anyway.” Both men wrote in the middle of actual disorder. Both refused to collapse.

The difference: Aurelius has cosmic order behind him — the Stoic logos, the rational structure of the universe. Camus has nothing. Only the person, alone.

The latter is lonelier. But it might be more honest.
